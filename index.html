The path to AI-ready

Most problems that arrive as AI requests are actually an earlier type in disguise. Work through each type in order — resolving an earlier type often eliminates the need to go further.

  1. 1
    Resolve first

    Ownership problem

    No one is clearly accountable. Tools don't fill ownership gaps — they make them more complicated. Name one owner before anything else.

  2. 2
    Then check

    Policy problem

    The rules are unclear or missing. Automating ambiguity doesn't resolve it — it just makes it faster. Write the policy before building anything.

  3. 3
    Then check

    Visibility problem

    The data exists but no one can see it. Requests come in via email, reports are built by hand, there's no unified view. A Qualtrics form and Tableau dashboard often solve what AI was being asked to solve.

  4. 4
    AI-ready ✓

    AI-ready problem

    Types 1–3 are resolved. The remaining gap involves synthesis, pattern recognition, or generation at scale — the work AI genuinely does well. Now the AI conversation can happen on solid ground.

Most problems that arrive as Type 4 requests are actually Type 1, 2, or 3. That's not a failure — it's useful information, and it's a solvable thing.

Right Tool First

Why this framework exists, and what it makes possible at NYU

NYU's schools and administrative units are doing real, meaningful work with AI. This framework exists to make more of that work succeed — by helping departments get to the right solution faster, whether that solution is AI or something else. The question it answers is not "should we use AI?" It's "what kind of challenge do we actually have?" Getting that right first is what makes the difference between an AI project that works and one that doesn't.

  • 01

    It meets schools where they are

    NYU's schools and administrative units are not at the same starting point — and that's fine. Right Tool First gives every unit a useful entry point, wherever they're starting from.

  • 02

    It gives innovators a clear path

    Across NYU, people are already experimenting — not because their job descriptions asked them to, but because they saw a challenge. This framework channels that energy productively.

  • 03

    It builds the foundation AI needs

    Most AI projects that struggle do so because of unclear ownership, missing policy, or inaccessible data — nothing to do with AI itself. Working through those first is what makes the AI work when you get there.

  • 04

    It protects against cost and risk

    A project that launches before it's ready doesn't just waste money — it makes the next conversation harder. Right Tool First helps ensure that when a school or unit commits to AI, it's a commitment worth making.

  • 05

    It creates equity across schools

    Without a framework, schools and units with natural AI champions race ahead while others are quietly left behind. This ensures the conditions for success reach every school and administrative unit.

  • 06

    It makes readiness a success

    A school or unit that resolves an ownership gap, clarifies a policy, or builds a dashboard it didn't have before hasn't failed to adopt AI. It's built the foundation that makes AI adoption durable when the time comes.

The four challenge types:  Type 1 — Ownership problem.  Type 2 — Policy problem.  Type 3 — Visibility problem.  Type 4 — AI-ready.  Most challenges that arrive as Type 4 requests are actually Type 1, 2, or 3 — and that's a solvable thing.
